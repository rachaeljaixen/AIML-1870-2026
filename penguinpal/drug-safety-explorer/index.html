About This Data

What is OpenFDA?

OpenFDA is a public API from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration that provides access to FDA datasets including adverse events, drug labeling, and recall enforcement reports.

Where does the data come from?

Adverse Events: The FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS) — a voluntary database where patients, healthcare providers, and manufacturers report drug side effects.

Drug Labeling: Official package insert information submitted by manufacturers to the FDA.

Recalls: FDA enforcement reports for products removed from the market.

Key Limitations

  • Voluntary reporting: Most adverse events are never reported — actual rates may be much higher
  • Correlation, not causation: A reported event doesn't mean the drug caused it
  • Data quality varies: Reports may be incomplete, duplicated, or inaccurate
  • Data lag: Recent events may not yet appear in the database
  • Not adjusted for exposure: Popular drugs will naturally have more reports

How to interpret results

Use this tool to get a general picture of reported safety information. Higher report counts do not necessarily mean a drug is more dangerous — they may reflect wider use or better reporting culture. Always discuss medication decisions with your healthcare provider.

Report adverse events to FDA

You can report adverse events through the FDA MedWatch Safety Reporting Portal.
